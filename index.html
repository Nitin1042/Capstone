History of Anime

The History of anime can be traced back to the start of the 20th century, with the earliest verifiable films dating from 1906.The first generation of animators in the late 1910s included Ōten Shimokawa, Jun'ichi Kōuchi and Seitaro Kitayama, commonly referred to as the "fathers" of anime.

In the 1980s which is said to be the Golden age of anime, anime became mainstream in Japan, experiencing a boom in production with the rise in popularity of anime like Gundam, Macross, Dragon Ball, and genres such as real robot, space opera and cyberpunk. Space Battleship Yamato and The Super Dimension Fortress Macross also achieved worldwide success after being adapted respectively as Star Blazers and Robotech.

And from then anime began its international journey and arrived at this position and beacame so popular, that people who watch anime on a daily basis were called as WEEBS.
